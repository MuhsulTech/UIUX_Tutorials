Understanding Users Through UX Research

UX Research helps you understand your users’ needs, motivations, frustrations, and behaviors. Without research, design becomes guesswork. With research, design becomes intentional.

User Insights Personas Journey Mapping

1. What is UX Research?

UX Research is the process of studying your users to understand their needs, behaviors, and pain points. It ensures that your design decisions are based on evidence, not assumptions.

Why UX Research matters
  • ◎ Reduces guesswork
  • ◎ Prevents usability issues early
  • ◎ Helps you design for real needs
  • ◎ Saves time and development cost
Research outputs
  • ✎ Personas
  • ✎ User journey maps
  • ✎ Problem statements
  • ✎ Insights & opportunities

2. Types of UX Research

UX Research can be divided into two main categories: qualitative and quantitative.

Qualitative Research
Understanding “why” users behave a certain way.
  • ◆ User interviews
  • ◆ Observations
  • ◆ Usability testing
  • ◆ Field studies
Quantitative Research
Understanding “what” users do at scale.
  • ◼ Surveys
  • ◼ Analytics
  • ◼ Heatmaps
  • ◼ A/B testing

3. Creating User Personas

Personas are fictional but realistic representations of your target users. They help you design with empathy.

Persona components
  • ◎ Name & background
  • ◎ Goals
  • ◎ Pain points
  • ◎ Behaviors
Why personas matter

They keep the team aligned on who you’re designing for. Every design decision should answer: “Does this help our persona achieve their goal?”

4. User Journey Mapping

A user journey map visualizes the steps a user takes to accomplish a goal, along with their emotions and pain points.

Journey stages
  • 1 Awareness
  • 2 Consideration
  • 3 Action
  • 4 Retention
What journey maps reveal

They highlight frustrations, opportunities, and moments where UX improvements can make the biggest impact.

5. Practical Exercise: Create 2 Personas

Create two personas for visitors of your portfolio website.

📝
Exercise
  1. Create Persona A: A recruiter looking for frontend developers.
  2. Create Persona B: A potential client wanting a website.
  3. Define their goals, frustrations, and motivations.
  4. Write one paragraph describing each persona’s behavior.
